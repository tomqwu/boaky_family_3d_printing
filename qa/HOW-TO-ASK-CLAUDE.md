# How to Ask Claude (for Peter & Matt) 🤖

Claude knows about our family, our printer, and our summer program — it reads
`CLAUDE.md` at the start of every session. You don't need to re-explain who you are.
Just say your name and ask.

## Good ways to start

- **"I'm Matt. My flexi dragon's legs fused together. What went wrong?"**
- **"I'm Peter. How do I turn a real map of our neighborhood into a printable model?"**
- **"We want a project for today, about 2 hours, using orange and black PLA."**
- **"Explain like I'm 10: why does the printer wipe its nozzle?"**

## Tips that get better answers

1. **Say what you already tried.** "I re-sliced with supports and it still failed"
   beats "it failed."
2. **Give numbers.** Layer height, filament type, how far into the print it broke.
3. **Photos help you describe.** Look at the failed print and describe what you see:
   stringy? shifted layers? spaghetti? gaps?
4. **Ask for the next step, not just the answer.** "What should I try first?"
5. **It's okay to ask "why" five times in a row.** That's the whole point of summer.

## What Claude will help with

- Troubleshooting failed prints (describe the symptom!)
- Finding project ideas from our [idea bank](../program/05-idea-bank.md) or MakerWorld
- Tinkercad / Fusion 360 design steps for something you imagined
- Planning multi-color prints and picking filaments
- Explaining how any part of the printer works

## What needs Dad

- Starting prints longer than bedtime
- Nozzle changes, repairs, or anything inside the printer
- Buying filament or tools
- Publishing anything online (MakerWorld accounts are 18+, so Dad posts)

## Leave a trail

If Claude helps you fix or learn something real, ask it to
**"add this to the Q&A log"** — it'll append a short note to
[`QA-LOG.md`](QA-LOG.md) so we never solve the same problem twice.
